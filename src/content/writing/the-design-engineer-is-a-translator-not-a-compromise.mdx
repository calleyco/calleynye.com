title: "The design engineer is a translator, not a compromise"
description: "The job at the seam between design and engineering is not to split the difference. It is to preserve intent while meeting real constraints."

Design-engineering work is often mischaracterized as "meeting in the middle." In practice, strong implementation requires translation without dilution.

The useful pattern is this: identify the non-negotiable user outcomes, map them to interaction and system constraints, and then implement with explicit tradeoffs.

That is where product quality and delivery speed stop fighting each other.
